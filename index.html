    • What Is Autism?

      According to the Autism Speaks website "Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and autism are both general terms for a group of complex disorders of brain development. These disorders are characterized, in varying degrees, by difficulties in social interaction, verbal and nonverbal communication and repetitive behaviors. With the May 2013 publication of the DSM-5 diagnostic manual, all autism disorders were merged into one umbrella diagnosis of ASD. Previously, they were recognized as distinct subtypes, including autistic disorder, childhood disintegrative disorder, pervasive developmental disorder-not otherwise specified (PDD-NOS) and Asperger syndrome.

       

      ASD can be associated with intellectual disability, difficulties in motor coordination and attention and physical health issues such as sleep and gastrointestinal disturbances. Some persons with ASD excel in visual skills, music, math and art.

       

      Autism appears to have its roots in very early brain development. However, the most obvious signs of autism and symptoms of autism tend to emerge between 2 and 3 years of age. Autism Speaks continues to fund research on effective methods for earlier diagnosis, as early intervention with proven behavioral therapies can improve outcomes. Increasing autism awareness is a key aspect of this work and one in which our families and volunteers play an invaluable role. "

    • About Autism Walk

      About Walk Now For Autism Speaks

      Autism Speaks Walk is the world’s largest fundraising event to support the diverse needs of the autism community. This grassroots movement is powered by parents of children on the autism spectrum, generating funds that fuel innovative research and make connections to critical lifelong supports and services.

       

      • FAQs

        Are pets allowed at the walk?

        No. Many affected children have a fear of animals and even the sweetest pet could negatively affect another participant.   Please be sensitive to this issue and leave your pets at home.  Only service dogs are permitted at the walk.

         

        Are strollers and wheelchairs allowed at the walk?

        Yes and even wagons for the little ones are welcome.

         

        Are bikes, rollerblades, scooters or skateboards allowed at the walk?

        For everyone's safety, we cannot allow any of these items.

    • Location

      Join Autism Speaks Los Angeles as we help raise money and awareness to help change the future for all of those who struggle with autism spectrum disorder. The walk will take place April 1, 2017 in Pasadena in the Rose Bowl Stadium.

    • Rose Bowl Directions

    • Parking

      • General Parking

        Parking in Lot H, Brookside Park and the Golf Course are sold by the City of Pasadena on game day.  Handicap parking is available immediately adjacent to the Rose Bowl Stadium, and vehicles must display a valid State of California handicapped placard and registration upon entering these lots.

      • Shuttle Buses

        Shuttle service to the stadium begins three (3) hours prior to the start of the event or kick-off. The shuttle service runs continuously until about one hour after the end of the event or game.

      • Parking Lot Policies

        • Overnight parking is prohibited in all Lots.
        • Parking lots close 90 minutes following the conclusion of the event or game.
        • Parking is first-come, first-served. No saving of parking spaces is permitted.
        • In and out privileges are not allowed.

      • RV Parking

        RV parking is located on West Drive south of Salvia Canyon and is available the day of the event or game day. The RV parking lot opens six hours prior to the event start time or kick-off.  Access to the RV parking area is by way of Linda Vista to Salvia Canyon, Pasadena.

      • Bus Parking

        The bus parking lot and bus parking rates vary for each event. The bus parking lot will open six (6) hours prior to the start of the event or kick-off.

    • The Walk

      On April 8, 2017, also known as World Autism Awareness Day, the 15th annual Autism Speaks Walk Los Angeles will take in the Rose Bowl Stadium to help raise crucial funds to support vital research, programs, and services that are changing the future for those who are impacted by autism.

       

       

       

       

       

       

       

      There is no registration fee for participating walkers.

       

       

       

       

       

       

       

      The Walk is 5k long but we also offer a shorter distance for participants!

      The rolling start begins at 9:30 am. Participants final time to start the walk is from 11:20am-12:15pm.

    • Entertainment on Stage

      We will have live entertainment from 8:00am - 1:00 pm

       

      8:00 - Welcoming Speech

      8:15- Live Band

      9:00 Holly Peete Speech

      9:30- Thanking the Walkers Speech

      9:45 - Asics CEO Speech

      10:30- Asics give away

      11:30- Live Band

      12:15- Closing Speech

       

    • Guest Speakers

      Founder Bob Wright will give an opening speech!

      Holly Peete and family will give a special speech!

      Asics' CEO Alistair Cameron will talk about why Asics has decided to sponsor this event.

    • Special Guest

      We will have special guest walking around greeting everyone! Feel free to take pictures with these special guest!
